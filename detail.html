Scientific Overview

The Andromeda Galaxy, also known as Messier 31, M31, or NGC 224 and originally the Andromeda Nebula, is a barred spiral galaxy approximately 2.5 million light-years from Earth and the nearest large galaxy to the Milky Way.

The galaxy's name stems from the area of Earth's sky in which it appears, the constellation of Andromeda, which itself is named after the Ethiopian princess who was the wife of Perseus in Greek mythology. The virial mass of the Andromeda Galaxy is of the same order of magnitude as that of the Milky Way, at 1 trillion solar masses (2.0×10⁴² kilograms).

Current estimates of the Andromeda Galaxy's diameter indicate it spans roughly 220,000 light-years, making it the largest member of the Local Group. It contains approximately one trillion stars, at least twice the number estimated for the Milky Way.
